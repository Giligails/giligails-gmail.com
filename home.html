Income from Salary, house property(property can be self-occupied or rented out), other sources(fixed deposits, savings account), capital gains(mutual funds or house property) and business and profession(other business/freelancing) is taxable.
(OR)

Excemptions

Certain part of income is excempted from taxation for salaried employees.

The excemption includes :

  • Amount gifted by others
  • HRA(Minimum of HRA Component of Salary, Actual Rent paid and 40% and 50% of [Basic+DA] in non metros and metros respectively)
  • Child Education Excemption for 1200 a year for a maximum of 2 Children
  • Convenience Allowance for Specially Abled Persons
  • Any losses incurred on Housing
Excemptions under u/s 10

Deductions under 80C

Any Investments/Savings for retirement / child Welfare Purposes upto a maximum of 1,50,000 per annum are excempted from taxation

The excemption includes :

  • Retirement related Investments like PF,Voluntary PF, PPF ..
  • Tax Saving Mutual Funds like ELSS
  • Tax Saving Government Bonds, Corporate Bonds .....
  • Unit linked Insurance Plans ,LIC , Child Welfare plans like Sukanya Samruddhi
  • Saving Schemes like NPS, NSC,
  • Housing Loan Principle paid (for first home buyers)
Deductions Under 80CCE

Other Deductions

Certain Other Expenditure is tax free

The expenditure following under this category are :

  • Medical Insurance Premium paid for self and family(spouse, children and parents)
  • Donations made to certain relief funds and charitable institutions
  • Interest paid on Educational Loan
  • NPS Contribution upto 50,000 in addition to 1,50,000 of 80C
  • Expenditure towards health Chekup for self and parents(based on eligibility)
  • Expenditure incurred for treatment od disease/disability of self/dependents
Deductions Excluded from 80CCE
